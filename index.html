Executive Summary

This report is part of the Telfer School of Management's Innovations in Education Series. It presents a synthesis of the findings, reflections and recommendations resulting from three co-construction workshops bringing together professors, students and pedagogical advisors around the same objective: to adapt the pedagogy of the B.Com in order to strengthen its coherence, participation and alignment with the needs of a changing world.

The exercise was not aimed at a simple reform of content, but at a cultural and institutional transformation. The discussions highlighted several converging challenges: the need for more collaborative academic governance, the need to support faculty pedagogical training, the redefinition of the classroom relationship, the coherence of the student pathway, as well as the ethical framework for the use of artificial intelligence.

Based on these observations, we formulate ten recommendations structured in five areas :

Axis 1: Innovation governance

Axis 2: Support for faculty

Axis 3: Pedagogical relationship

Axis 4: Student experience

Axis 5: Artificial intelligence

These orientations reflect a common desire: to make the Telfer B.Com a training model that balances academic excellence, pedagogical innovation and educational humanism.

Beyond reforms, this report proposes a sustainable vision: that of a school where teaching, learning and collaboration become shared acts, driven by trust, responsibility and creativity. The renewed B.Com aims to train managers capable not only of performing, but also of thinking and acting meaningfully in a changing world.

Introduction

The revision of the Telfer School of Management's Bachelor of Commerce (B.Com) program is part of a collective process of pedagogical transformation initiated as part of the Telfer Innovations in Education Series.

The objective was not to produce a technical or curricular reform, but to push the boundaries of pedagogy by rethinking the way of teaching, learning and collaborating. This initiative, supported by the faculty's management, brought together professors, students, academic advisors and staff members in the same space for reflection and co-construction.

The first year of the B.Com served as a starting point, perceived as a founding moment where students' reference points, motivation and sense of belonging are forged. The discussions revealed a shared observation: beyond the content and methods, it is the pedagogical relationship between professors, students and institutions that determines the quality of the learning experience.

The workshops made it possible to name these issues, to understand their causes and to imagine realistic solutions, anchored in everyday academic life.

This reflection was based on three ambitions:

Restore the coherence of the training path and give a clear meaning to each stage of the program.

To strengthen trust and collaboration between actors, by promoting continuous and constructive dialogue.

Foster a culture of innovation and responsibility, particularly in the use of technology and artificial intelligence.

The three successive workshops thus constituted a laboratory of living ideas, where the diversity of voices; those of professors, students and advisors, made it possible to identify specific findings and applicable recommendations.

Each exchange contributed to the emergence of a common vision: that of a school where pedagogy becomes a lever for meaning, coherence and humanism.

This report presents a synthesis of this work and proposes structuring recommendations to guide the transformation of the B.Com.

It reflects the very spirit of Telfer: an institution that looks to the future, is committed to excellence, and believes that quality education is born of dialogue, trust and the courage to innovate together.

Axis 1: Innovation governance

R01
1.1 Create a Student-Faculty Innovation Committee
Full text Quotes

Report

Communication between students and professors remains fragmented, limited to occasional exchanges.

"We feel that there are decisions that are made without us really understanding why, or without being able to give our opinion." - Student, Workshop 2

This sentiment reflects a shared need for transparency and more structured dialogue within the education community. The teachers, for their part, insisted on the need for a structured forum for consultation, guaranteeing the coherence of the programme, the circulation of information and the continuity of pedagogical orientations.

Vision

To build a B.Com aligned with the real needs of its community, it is essential to institutionalize a regular and transparent dialogue between students and professors. The creation of a joint committee on pedagogy aims to make this consultation an ongoing process of listening and continuous improvement, rather than a one-time exercise. This committee will become a co-construction space where student perspectives and faculty expertise complement each other, making it possible to identify emerging issues and enrich the quality of the learning experience for the entire Telfer community.

Recommendation

Establish a standing and representative committee to:

Monitor the implementation of educational reforms;

Ensure consistency in learning experiences;

Guarantee the transparency of decisions and guidelines;

Formulate proposals based on data and feedback.

Implementation

Composition: 3 professors, 3 students, 1 pedagogical advisor, 1 representative of the dean's office.

Mandate: monitoring of reforms and evaluation of their effects.

Timeframe: Semi-annual meetings, public report to the Programme Committee.

Role and impact

The students contribute their experience, the professors their expertise, the advisor and the dean's office guarantee institutional alignment. This shared framework will establish sustainable co-responsibility, strengthening trust, commitment and pedagogical quality.

The committee will become a structuring engine of the B.Com, guaranteeing the coherence and continuity of reforms.

1.2 Adopt a common pedagogical quality guide
Full text Quotes

Report

  • The workshops revealed a notable diversity in the structure and presentation of the courses, particularly between the English and French sections.
  • "In the English version of the same course, the plan is clear, complete, the expectations are precise... whereas in French, you sometimes feel like you're taking another course. »
  • Student, Workshop 1
  • This variation, often resulting from each teacher's own pedagogical styles, reflects a great wealth of approaches, but it can also create perceived differences in the clarity or fairness of expectations. The professors also expressed the desire to have a common frame of reference that would promote better coherence between the sections, while preserving the pedagogical freedom and creativity that are the strength of Telfer's faculty.

Vision

To provide every student, regardless of their section or language of instruction, with a consistent, equitable and inspiring learning experience. The Telfer Pedagogical Quality Guide would serve as a shared benchmark, facilitating the harmonization of practices while valuing the diversity of teaching approaches and styles. Rather than a binding framework, it would be a tool of reference and collaboration, supporting the clarity, transparency and creativity of the faculty, in the service of collective pedagogical excellence.

Recommendation

Implement a Telfer Teacher Quality Guide as a common reference for all courses in the B.Com that would define:

A harmonized structure of the lesson plans;

Clear standards for Brightspace and student communication;

Shared principles of evaluation and pedagogical monitoring.

Implementation

Development: a joint teacher/student working group, supported by the Pedagogical Service, designs a clear, flexible guide adapted to the realities of the courses.

Content: learning objectives, teaching methods, evaluation criteria, participation expectations.

Digital standardization: Adoption of Brightspace standards to ensure a smooth and fair experience.

Follow-up: annual review by the committee to maintain the relevance of the guide.

Role and impact

The pedagogical direction ensures coordination, the professors co-construct and enrich the content, the students participate in the consultations, and the Pedagogical Service supports the implementation.

This guide will become a collaborative tool for quality and equity, ensuring consistency of B.Com and strengthening Telfer's academic reputation.

Axis 2: Support for faculty

R02
2.1 Developing pedagogical competence as a pillar of excellence
Full text Quotes

Report

The workshops highlighted a shared need: to fully recognize pedagogy as an essential dimension of the teaching profession.

"The teachers know their subject very well, but sometimes you feel that they have trouble transmitting." - Student, Workshop 3

Telfer faculty have outstanding disciplinary expertise and a strong commitment to their students. However, the rapid evolution of teaching practices and educational technologies is creating a gap between mastery of content and mastery of transmission. Doctoral students, in particular, represent an important asset, but require structured support to transform their theoretical skills into effective pedagogical practices.

Vision

Make pedagogical competence a pillar of professional development at Telfer. The aim is to offer each teacher, whether experienced or at the beginning of the course, the means to enrich their practice, stimulate student engagement and adapt their teaching to an ever-changing learning environment. Pedagogy thus becomes a skill that is valued, supported and recognized in the same way as research and service to the community.

Recommendation

Launch the Telfer Teaching Excellence program, a faculty certification pathway in university pedagogy, mandatory for doctoral lecturers and open to all faculty.

Implementation

Three additional modules:

Active pedagogy and student engagement.

Digital innovation and hybrid learning.

Inclusion, equity and intercultural communication.

Cross-mentoring between experienced teachers and new teachers.

Valuation of the program in the evaluation and progression criteria.

Role and impact

The Pedagogical Service coordinates the training, the professors contribute as mentors and the doctoral students develop their key skills. This program will strengthen an engaged and collaborative teaching culture, positioning Telfer as a benchmark for modern academic pedagogy.

2.2 Creating a Faculty Community of Innovation and Ongoing Support
Full text Quotes

Report

The exchanges between teachers highlighted a common need: to preserve the freedom of teaching while promoting the sharing of good practices.

"Everyone teaches in their own way, which is also what makes us who we are. But we always benefit from discussing our ways of transmitting. - Student, Workshop 3

Teachers value their autonomy, which reflects their style and experience, but also express a desire to work together better to enrich each other. The diversity of approaches, however, when not accompanied by a structured dialogue, can create gaps in perception and coherence between sections.

Vision

Promote an open and learning teaching culture, where innovation does not replace freedom, but nurtures it. The aim is to offer teachers a collective framework where they can exchange, be inspired and continuously improve their practices, while asserting their uniqueness.

Recommendation

To set up a Telfer Pedagogical Forum, both a platform and a meeting space, to allow professors to exchange, experiment and collaboratively develop concrete initiatives for pedagogical improvement. Create the Telfer Pedagogical Forum, a collaborative space where teachers share their experiences, explore new methods and co-construct pedagogical tools adapted to the reality of the B.Com.

Implementation

Thematic meetings : monthly workshops on topics such as student motivation, equity in evaluation or active approaches, with shared summaries in a common space.

Living Resource Bank : an internal platform with sample courses, interactive activities and inspiring feedback.

Valorization : institutional recognition of contributions and funding of projects resulting from the Forum.

Role and impact

Teachers are the driving force behind the Forum: they share their know-how and discover new approaches. The Pedagogical Service acts as a facilitator, and the management supports the dissemination and recognition of initiatives.

This system will promote a collective dynamic where diversity becomes a strength, and where each teacher, in his or her own style, contributes to shared excellence.

Axis 3: Pedagogical relationship

R03
3.1 Making the classroom a space for collective learning
Full text Quotes

Report

The workshops revealed a shared observation: the traditional lecture course struggles to attract attention and create a real learning link.

"Students are no less capable than before, they learn differently. We have to agree to move away from the transmissive model. - Teacher, Workshop 2

Students arrive at university with more interactive and digital learning habits. However, they often find themselves in formats that are not very participatory, where attendance in class sometimes seems symbolic. Teachers, for their part, wish to renew a more lively dialogue and give back to teaching its dimension of exchange and collective construction.

Vision

The classroom must once again become a living space for co-learning, where the transmission of knowledge becomes a shared construction. It is less a question of breaking with tradition than of restoring meaning, rhythm and reciprocity to the act of teaching. This approach is based on a pedagogy of mutual commitment: the teacher guides and inspires, the group collaborates, the student acts and reflects. Together, they create an active learning dynamic, based on curiosity, participation and mutual respect.

Recommendation

Reinvent the format of first-year courses in order to promote interaction, critical reflection and practice, while preserving high standards and teachers' mastery of the content.

Implementation

Structuring of courses and dynamic tools: each session follows a short cycle integrating exposition, application and discussion, supported by simple participatory tools (anonymous surveys, cross-discussions) that link theory to practice.

Personalized support: the Pedagogical Service offers technical and creative support to script courses and design interactive activities.

Continuous valorization: Innovative teachers present their initiatives at faculty meetings, contributing to a shared culture of experimentation and continuous improvement.

Role and impact

Teachers remain the pillars of this transformation: free in their style, they are supported in their innovation processes. Students become active partners in the learning process, responsible for their participation and feedback. Finally, the administration is accompanying this change with measures of recognition and concrete support.

This dynamic will make the classroom a place of authentic, stimulating and inclusive exchange, where learning becomes a collective, concrete and sustainable experience, true to the Telfer spirit.

R03
3.2 Fostering a culture of continuous feedback and academic trust
Full text Quotes

Report

The workshops highlighted a major need: to establish a more open, reciprocal and continuous communication between teachers and students.

"We often have the impression that our comments are only read at the end, when it's too late for it to change anything." - Student, Workshop 3

"End-of-term assessments are helpful, but not to improve the course along the way." - Teacher, Workshop 2

The lack of regular feedback maintains a perception gap: students feel little heard, while teachers lack concrete indicators to adjust their approach in real time. This lack of exchange creates an avoidable distance, where constant dialogue could strengthen engagement and the quality of learning.

Vision

Foster a culture of continuous feedback, based on trust and collaboration. Feedback must become a shared reflex, perceived not as a judgment, but as a means of collective learning.

By establishing regular and benevolent exchanges, the faculty reinforces transparency, co-responsibility and pedagogical equity. This dynamic will make it possible to transform feedback into a real driver of improvement and innovation.

Recommendation

Implement an institutional, active, feedback-based system integrated with Brightspace that is accessible, confidential, and action-oriented to support rapid and visible adjustments throughout the semester.

Implementation

Monthly pedagogical surveys: short anonymous questionnaires (3 to 5 questions) about pace, clarity and workload. The results, which are confidential, are only used by the teacher to adjust his pedagogy in a constructive way.

Communication of adjustments: at the beginning of the next session, the teacher shares the changes decided following the feedback ("You mentioned that..." So we'll... "), consolidating the confidence and credibility of the process.

Role and impact

Teachers initiate and maintain dialogue, creating a climate of respect and listening. Students contribute with sincere and constructive feedback, promoting continuous improvement. The Pedagogical Service acts as a facilitator, while the management ensures that this practice is valued and institutionalized.

This system will bring about a learning relationship based on mutual trust and responsiveness. Courses will become more adaptive, inclusive, and relevant, and feedback will become an enduring pillar of educational quality at Telfer.

Axis 4: Student experience

R04
4.1 Design a year structured around meaning and progression
Full text Quotes

Report

The workshops revealed a recurring difficulty: the first year of the B.Com is perceived as fragmented, without a clear common thread.

"It feels like you're going from one course to another without understanding where it's all leading." - Student, Workshop 3

Newcomers, whether they come from CEGEP, high school or abroad, mention a lack of reference points and coherence in the transition to university. Several teachers have observed a drop in commitment linked to this fragmentation.

Vision

The first year must become a clear, coherent and formative foundation, giving meaning to each apprenticeship. It must guide students in their academic adaptation while strengthening their understanding of the program and its purposes.

The objective is not to add content, but to articulate the courses around a common thread: to gradually develop the analytical, ethical and collaborative skills that are the basis of the B.Com's identity.

Recommendation

Create a credited integration course "Living and Learning at Telfer", accompanied by a progressive redesign of the first-year courses to ensure better pedagogical continuity and a smooth transition to university life.

Implementation

Integration course: mandatory and credited, it will serve as an initial benchmark for new students. It will cover academic methodology, time management, intercultural communication, academic ethics and the discovery of faculty resources.

Harmonization of the pathway: The Program Committee will define a clear framework for progression between first-year courses, aligning key competencies (analysis, communication, collaboration, accountability). A correlation table will make it possible to better articulate learning and avoid redundancies.

Role and impact

First-year teachers will become the architects of this coherence, integrating cross-curricular learning into their courses. Students will benefit from clear support and a tangible sense of progress. The administration will guarantee the cohesion of the system and will ensure follow-up based on indicators of success and satisfaction.

This redesign will make the first year a foundational experience, where each student understands not only what they are learning, but also why they are learning it; thus strengthening motivation, confidence and a sense of belonging to the Telfer community.

R04
4.2 Develop an integrated and self-directed learning environment
Full text Quotes

Report

Students face a persistent challenge: navigating between many platforms, services, and resources that are often disconnected.

"We often discover by chance tools that could have helped us weeks before. Everything is there, but nothing is really accessible. - Student, Workshop 3

This digital dispersion creates an invisible barrier to engagement. The resources exist, but their lack of integration reduces their scope. Professors also express the desire to have a centralized space to better communicate, monitor student feedback and promote educational initiatives.

Vision

The Telfer experience must extend beyond the classroom, into a coherent, intuitive and lively digital environment, designed with and for students. Technology is becoming a lever for autonomy, collaboration and shared management. The aim is to enable students to shape a space in their image, where learning, engagement and innovation meet.

Recommendation

Develop the "Telfer Essentials" platform, integrated with Brightspace and administered by a Student Digital Coordination Committee, with the support of the Academic Department. This platform will bring together academic resources, support services, clubs, events and faculty initiatives into a unified ecosystem.

Implementation

Student co-design and governance: a joint student-faculty-advisor group defines the architecture and functional priorities. The student committee then ensures the day-to-day management (updates, moderation, liaison with faculty services), while the teachers contribute as content partners.

Participatory governance: every six months, the committee publishes an activity report and organises a consultation with users to adjust the functionalities. This model ensures the continued relevance of the platform and trains students in collaborative digital project management.

Role and impact

Students become actors and managers of their learning environment. Teachers participate in the dissemination of content and the promotion of pedagogical innovations. Finally, the administration ensures the coherence, security and accessibility of the system.

"Telfer Essentials" will be the digital backbone of faculty life: a space for autonomy, mutual aid and experimentation. By designing and facilitating their own learning environment, students will learn management by practicing it, thus solidifying Telfer's vocation as a learning and connected school.

Axis 5: Artificial intelligence

R05
5.1 Supervise and empower the use of AI in learning
Full text Quotes

Report

The workshops highlighted a strong consensus: artificial intelligence is profoundly transforming the way we learn.

"We use it to structure our ideas, but we never know if it's allowed or not." - Student, Workshop 3

Students see AI as a support for thinking and productivity, while many teachers express concern about preserving intellectual integrity and academic equity. The lack of a common framework feeds a grey area between innovation and failure, trust and control.

Vision

The objective is to train students capable of using AI as a tool for discernment, analysis and innovation, while consolidating the intellectual rigor and critical thinking that are the basis of university learning.

Recommendation

Adopt a Faculty Policy for the Use of Artificial Intelligence, based on accountability, transparency and critical thinking.

Implementation

Clear and evolving framework of use: the Joint Student-Faculty Committee will define three levels of use:

Allowed: reformulation, structuring, translation.

Box: partial generation of content, provided that it is explicitly stated.

Forbidden: complete substitution of reasoning or intellectual production.

Each work involving AI will include a standardized mention:

"AI assistance used for rephrasing or organizing ideas."

Digital Critical Thinking Training: integrated into the Living and Learning course at Telfer, this training will address responsible uses, algorithmic biases and the boundary between assistance and plagiarism.

Support for teachers: provision of support to adapt assessments to the age of AI, by valuing personal reflection, oral work and practical cases.

Role and impact

Teachers guide and supervise the use of AI in a spirit of coherence and benevolence. Students are committed to responsible and transparent use. The administration ensures the uniform dissemination of the framework, supported by the Pedagogical Service for training and technical support. This policy will establish a culture of shared digital responsibility, where AI becomes a lever for informed learning, not a threat to academic integrity. It will help position Telfer as an innovative, clear-sighted and ethical school in the way it integrates emerging technologies.

R05
5.2 Building a Culture of Integrity
Full text Quotes

Report

The challenge of artificial intelligence goes beyond technology: it affects academic culture and the very meaning of learning.

"We must move from a logic of control to a logic of trust and responsibility." - Professor, Workshop 2

The workshops revealed a persistent tension: the majority of students use AI not to bend the rules, but for fear of doing the wrong thing or because of a lack of reference points. Teachers, for their part, seek to reconcile academic requirements and pedagogical openness. This highlights a critical need: to rebuild trust and recognize that integrity is a skill to be developed, not just an obligation to be respected.

Vision

To make integrity a shared and living value, anchored in all dimensions of the B.Com. The aim is to entrench a culture where ethics, critical thinking and responsibility accompany every learning. AI then becomes a tool for intellectual maturity, not a risk to be controlled. Telfer must embody a school where trust is the basis of rigor and where success is as much about knowledge as it is about probity.

Recommendation

Implement an Enhanced Integrity Pact, supported by workshops, resources and institutional support, in order to sustainably strengthen the culture of academic ethics and trust.

Implementation

Institutionalization of the Integrity Pact: symbolically signed by all new students, it will recall the values of responsibility, honesty and intellectual respect, while clarifying the rules for the use of AI.

Digital integrity workshops: organized each session, they will bring together professors, mentors and student ambassadors around concrete cases and ethical dilemmas. These spaces will encourage dialogue, reflection and learning by example.

Confidential support: a faculty platform will offer students the opportunity to consult resources or advisors before a breach occurs, favouring prevention over sanctions.

Valuing Honest Behaviour: Symbolic recognition will highlight exemplary initiatives of integrity and collaboration.

Role and impact

Teachers embody integrity through exemplarity and benevolence. Students become ambassadors, in Professors embody integrity through their consistency and benevolence. Students become ambassadors for them, adopting transparent and accountable practices. The Pedagogical Service coordinates the implementation, and the management ensures that these principles are embedded in the institutional culture. This approach will make integrity a hallmark of the Telfer School of Management's identity, a faculty where trust, rigor and ethics nurture academic excellence over time.

Conclusion

The work carried out through the workshops was not intended to correct a curriculum, but to reinvent the way we teach, learn and collaborate.

The transformation of the B.Com is based on a clear ambition: to train minds capable of discernment, rigour and openness, in a world where information is abundant but meaning is scarce. She recognizes that the quality of an education is not measured solely by the transmission of knowledge, but by an institution's ability to cultivate curiosity, critical thinking and intellectual ethics.

The teachers expressed their desire to give meaning to their teaching, to create spaces for dialogue and to nurture critical thinking. The students, for their part, have shown a desire to learn differently, to understand the why behind the how, and to actively participate in the evolution of the program.

By bringing these two voices together, Telfer is reconnecting with its primary vocation: to make knowledge a living, shared and demanding experience.

Reinventing the first year of the B.Com means giving coherence and breath to the course. It means making technology a tool for discernment, not ease, and integrity a collective strength, not a constraint. It is to affirm that pedagogical modernity is not based on rupture, but on a renewed alliance between teachers and students, united by the search for meaning and the passion for learning.

This reform commits each member of the community to contribute to a culture of trust, listening and shared excellence. It calls for a pedagogy of courage, where innovation is aligned with the values of responsibility and intellectual rigour, and where success becomes a common work.

The Telfer School of Management is thus positioned as a clear-sighted and committed institution, which does not undergo change but shapes it. By training managers capable of understanding, questioning and acting responsibly, it fulfills the promise of the renewed B.Com:

learn to think about the world in order to better transform it.
